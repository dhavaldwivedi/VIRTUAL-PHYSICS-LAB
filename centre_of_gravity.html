Theory

When an irregular lamina is suspended freely from a point, it rotates until its centre of gravity lies vertically below the point of suspension. A plumb line hung from the same point gives that vertical direction. If the lamina is suspended from two or more different holes, the vertical traces intersect at the centre of gravity.

Procedure

1. Suspend the lamina from one hole.
2. Observe the plumb line hanging vertically downward.
3. Draw the trace of the plumb line on the lamina.
4. Repeat from another hole.
5. The intersection of the traces gives the centre of gravity.
