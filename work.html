Show Theory

Why cos(θ) appears in work

Work depends on the component of force along the displacement. Since the block moves along the incline, only the along-the-incline part of the applied force contributes to W = F s cos(θ).

Why h = s sin(θ)

The block travels along the sloping side of a right triangle. The vertical rise is the opposite side, so height is the displacement times sin(θ).

Why gravitational work uses mgh

Gravity depends only on the vertical change, not the path length. Once height is known, the work done against gravity is Wg = m g h.

Why timing matters

The applied force acts only between the chosen start and stop times. A longer force interval gives more time for acceleration, so the block reaches a different displacement, speed, and final height.
